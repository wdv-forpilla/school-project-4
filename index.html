Welcome to Baguio City

Burnham Park

Considered as the city's "mother of all parks"

is an open green park at the heart of Baguio.

The park's lush greeneries and blooming flowers make you forget that you are in the middle of a highly urbanized city.

Baguio Cathedral

Formally known as the Our Lady of Atonement Cathedral

The Baguio Cathedral can be reached by climbing a 104-step staircase.

Wright Park

is considerably smaller than Burnham Park, but it is also less crowded and more peaceful. It is located in the eastern part of Baguio and fronts the main gate of The Mansion.

There you'll find several horses you can hire and ride around in the vicinity. If you're traveling with kids, good news! There are friendly ponies that are perfect for children.

MinesView Park

Baguio was a former mining town in the early 20th century.

Mines View Park offers a front-row view of the Cordillera Mountains and Benguet's gold and copper mines. You will also find several shops selling succulents, flowers, bonsai, as well as woven, knitted, and wooden souvenirs.

Strawberry Farms in La Trinidad

just outside of Baguio is where visitors can enjoy the experience of harvesting strawberries for themselves during the harvest season. The best time to visit in order to go strawberry picking is from March to April.
